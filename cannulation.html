About Nasogastric Tube Insersion

Insertion of a Nasogastric (NG) Tube:

  1. Prepare the Patient: Have the patient tilt their head slightly forward and swallow water if possible. This helps guide the tube into the esophagus rather than the trachea.
  2. Insert the Tube: Gently insert the NG tube into one of the patient’s nostrils, directing it toward the back of the throat.
  3. Advance During Swallowing: Advance it steadily as the patient swallows to help pass the tube down into the esophagus.
  4. Monitor Patient Comfort: Continue to advance the tube, checking the patient’s comfort and ensuring that it is not causing distress.
  5. Secure the Tube: Once the tube reaches the stomach, secure the tube with tape at the patient’s nose, ensuring it is not too tight to avoid irritation.
